This repository builds and evaluates a reinforcement-learning stock trading system.

Primary purpose:
- Train a PPO agent on a custom Gymnasium trading environment over a basket of large-cap tech stocks.
- Evaluate the agent less as a raw profit maximizer and more as a buy/sell signal generator with measurable directional accuracy.
- Run repeatable experiment sweeps over reward-shaping and PPO hyperparameters.
- Inspect results through a Streamlit dashboard that covers signal analytics, experiment execution, and experiment-history interpretation.

How the system works:
- `src/market_data.py` downloads OHLCV data from Yahoo Finance for a fixed tech basket, normalizes it, aggregates it into a date-level training frame, and optionally merges daily news sentiment features.
- `src/news_data.py` fetches Yahoo Finance news, scores sentiment with heuristic/Ollama/Gemini providers, caches article sentiment, and produces ticker-day sentiment features.
- `src/trading_env.py` defines the core Gymnasium environment. The action space is `Hold / Long / Short`, and the reward is a shaped combination of realized portfolio return, directional alignment, hold penalties, anti-collapse action bonus, and drawdown penalty. The reward logic is explicitly written to avoid look-ahead bias.
- `src/train_bot.py` is the basic training entry point. It loads the training data, creates `TradingEnv`, trains a PPO policy with Stable Baselines3, and saves the resulting model.
- `src/signal_analytics.py` loads a trained PPO model, simulates actions over a dataset, labels each step with forward returns for evaluation only, and computes metrics such as actionable accuracy, buy/sell precision-recall, trade win rate, and cumulative signal return.
- `src/experiments.py` is the experiment engine. It performs walk-forward train/validation/test splits, trains many PPO runs across seed and hyperparameter combinations, evaluates them on validation and test windows, compares them with a QQQ benchmark, and writes leaderboard and snapshot outputs.
- `src/analytics_dashboard.py` is the operator UI. It exposes three workflows: single-model signal analysis, experiment execution, and experiment-insight pages that read snapshot history and generate next-run recommendations.

What this codebase is really optimizing for:
- Better actionable trading signals on held-out data.
- Lower collapse rate across seeds.
- Better validation-to-test transfer.
- Reward functions and training settings that remain realistic after removing look-ahead bias.

Key repository assets:
- `data/` contains cached datasets, sentiment caches, leaderboard outputs, and timestamped experiment snapshots.
- `models/` contains saved PPO models.
- `tests/` focuses on smoke tests plus reward/look-ahead validation.
- `docs/` records design notes, reward-fix details, sentiment integration, GPU notes, and project plans.
- `notebooks/` support exploration and usage walkthroughs.

In short, this is not just a simple RL trading bot demo. It is an RL trading research workspace centered on training a PPO long/short/neutral policy, measuring the quality of its generated signals, and iterating on reward design and hyperparameters through dashboard-driven experiment loops.
