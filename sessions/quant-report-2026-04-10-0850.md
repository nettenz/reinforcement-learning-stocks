## Executive Summary
After identifying a "cost-blindness" and "risk-paralysis" trap in previous runs, we pivoted to **Reward Realism Phase 2**. By forcing the agent to account for 10bps transaction costs and rebalancing the reward magnitude away from drawdown penalties (79% -> 39%), we achieved the first positive alpha result on the NVDA test set.

## Analysis of Variants

### Variant A: Conservative (Winner)
- **Result:** Promotion Candidate.
- **Why it worked:** Zero action bonus forced the agent to find real edge instead of "farming" bonuses. The 39% drawdown penalty provided enough safety without suppressing alpha capture.
- **Verdict:** Best balance of stability and performance.

### Variant B: Balanced (High Signal)
- **Result:** Accuracy Pass (0.58), but Alpha Fail.
- **Why it failed:** Despite the highest accuracy, it over-traded (incurring 16% cost drag). The lower drawdown penalty (7%) allowed too much churn.
- **Verdict:** Promising for signal generation, but needs higher turnover penalties.
